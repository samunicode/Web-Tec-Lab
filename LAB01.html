⦿ Mall Road, Shimla

Mall Road in Shimla is a bustling hub of activity, lined with shops, restaurants, and cafes. The road is the main shopping area in the city and is always crowded with locals and tourists alike. Visitors can find everything from traditional Himachali souvenirs to modern fashion and accessories on this street. The road is also home to several historical buildings, including the Christ Church and the Town Hall. The beautiful views of the Himalayas in the background make for a picturesque stroll along the Mall Road.

⦿ Taj Mahal, Agra

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. The intricate details and intricate carvings on the building make it a true masterpiece of Mughal architecture. Visitors can also enjoy the beautiful gardens surrounding the Taj Mahal, making it a popular tourist destination. It stands as a symbol of love and devotion, drawing millions of visitors from around the world every year.

⦿ Hawa Mahal, Jaipur

The Hawa Mahal is a palace in the city of Jaipur, India. Built from red and pink sandstone, it is on the edge of the City Palace, Jaipur, and extends to the Zenana, or women's chambers.The structure was built in 1799 by the Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, grandson of Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh, the founder of the city of Jaipur, India. He was so inspired by the unique structure of Khetri Mahal that he built this grand and historical palace.

⦿ Bhangarh Fort, Bhangarh

Bhangarh Fort is a 17th century fort located in Bhangarh, Rajasthan, India. It is considered one of the most haunted places in India and is said to be cursed by a local sorcerer. The fort is known for its intricate architecture and beautiful temples, but also has a dark history of death and tragedy. Visitors are not allowed to enter the fort after sunset due to the eerie atmosphere. The fort and its precincts are well preserved.
